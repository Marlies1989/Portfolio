Projects

Website design Justice & Peace, from Wordpress to Craft.CMS

Justice & Peace Netherlands is a non-profit organisation dedicated to defending and promoting respect for human rights and social justice for everyone. Their current website is based on WordPress. For their new website, they switched to Two Kings so they are not depending on one person only and they also updated their branding.

Based on their new brand identity, meetings we had with their team, the old website and our ideas, I made a Figma prototype. The prototype's modular design allows for flexible page creation, empowering the client to construct diverse page layouts. After the prototype was finished I could start developing the static templates based on HTML / CSS / Javascript.

There were some challenges, a big menu, and a title gradient with a white background, but with the help of colleagues and the internet, I managed to create the templates based on the design.

A new contemporary look - Logement Dwarsgracht.

Logement Dwarsgracht is a small guesthouse in the middle of the Weerribben Wieden. The current website was launched in 2008 and since then, no update has been done. At that time, websites were not built responsively, in the current era this is indispensable. In addition to the desire for a responsive website, the design could also use an update to a more modern design.

It is a simple website that does not need to do much, for this reason, I chose to develop it with Vue.js. I wanted to learn a new js framework and during my research, I ended up with Vue.js.

There were no limitations to the design, so I opted for a minimalist design. The target group of Logement Dwarsgracht is often 50+, and a simple minimalist website suits this better. Through the use of colour and font, the website radiates peace and luxury.

The website is not yet live but is deployed through Netlify. This was chosen so the client can experience the new website the same as their target group, test it and provide feedback.

Website design for The Hague Humanity Hub based on their new branding.

The Hague Humanity Hub supports and strengthens the ecosystem for a more peaceful and just world. Their current website was built with WordPress, and different plugins were added through different people. In the end, nobody knows which plugins were used for what. To end the many plugins they choose Two Kings to rebuild their new website with Bolt CMS. Besides that, they also updated their branding, so a redesign was also needed.

Based on their new branding, meetings and our ideas we have developed a prototype with Figma. After the prototype was approved I started developing the static templates, based on HTML / CSS / Javascript and for animations, I used GSAP.

Due to circumstances, the client decided to take another approach for their new website. Therefore unfortunately these static templates were not necessary anymore. But still, I am happy with the result.

Showcase your art - website design and Craft template

In my spare time, I love to make illustrations, often illustrations of holidays I have made as a memory. These illustrations are made with Adobe Illustrator. Friends, family and new people around me are curious about what these look like. For this reason, I developed this website.

This website is built with HTML/CSS/Javascript. It is a simple design so the attention is focused on the illustrations. The illustrations can also be displayed by category and each category will be added to the navigation menu.

The current website online is based on HTML / CSS / Javascript, but I have also developed this template in Craft.cms. At Two Kings, a new website is developed with Craft.cms. To gain more knowledge and learn this cms system, I have converted this design into a Craft.cms template.
